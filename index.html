Hello,

I am a PhD Candidate at University of Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne (CES), working under the supervision of Clément Bosquet. I hold an MSc from Toulouse School of Economics.

My research focuses on urban, real estate, and financial economics, building upon several years of experience in the banking and real estate sectors. It combines spatial analysis and applied econometrics to produce causal evidence relevant to policy makers and real estate professionals.

In my PhD dissertation, I study major transformations that reshape cities through the lens of corporate real estate markets: the rise of remote work, building conversions to housing, and the energy transition.

I recently visited the Department of Geography at London School of Economics and the Spatial Economics Department at VU Amsterdam.

Research

Working Paper

Remote Work and the Intracity Dynamics of Office Markets: Evidence from Paris

This paper examines the intracity effect of Working From Home (WFH) on office markets. I develop an exogenous municipality-level WFH exposure indicator in the Paris metropolitan area and leverage on the Covid-19 shock as a natural experiment to implement a difference-in-differences strategy. Using a unique dataset on commercial real estate, findings reveal a significant and growing rise in durable office vacancy due to remote working, with effects more pronounced in areas with larger firms and further from the city center. This indicates a relative preference for central locations with better market access, which become more affordable through office downsizing. This shift exacerbates spatial disparities, disadvantaging some peripheral business districts. Furthermore, the paper sheds light on the negative externalities of office oversupply. In areas most exposed to WFH, prices and transactions decline substantially, suggesting persistent market anticipations. I also find evidence of spillover effects, with employment losses in nearby retail and restaurant sectors.
